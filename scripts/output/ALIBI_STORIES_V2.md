## STORY 1: THE LANGFORD NECKLACE

**Tone**: Classic Crime — glamorous, cinematic

---

### CRIME SCENARIO
*Shown on TV at game start. All players see this.*

The Langford Diamond Necklace — worth half a million — was the centrepiece of Saturday night's charity gala at the Grand Hotel. By midnight it was gone, lifted from its locked display case while two hundred guests danced in the next room. Hotel security found the case open, undamaged, and empty. Whoever took it had a key.

---

### GUILTY BRIEF
*Private. Only the Guilty player reads this.*

You took the necklace. You've been working at the hotel for three months — long enough to get a copy of the display case key from the night manager's office. At 11:40pm, during the band's final set, you slipped into the exhibition room, unlocked the case, pocketed the necklace, and walked straight out through the kitchen exit. The necklace is currently inside a hollowed-out book in your car boot. At 4+ players: your accomplice is [ACCOMPLICE NAME] — they kept the kitchen corridor clear for you.

---

### WITNESS BRIEF
*Private. Only the Witness player reads this. They do NOT know they are a Witness.*

The Langford Diamond Necklace — worth half a million — was the centrepiece of Saturday night's charity gala at the Grand Hotel. By midnight it was gone, lifted from its locked display case while two hundred guests danced in the next room. Hotel security found the case open, undamaged, and empty. Whoever took it had a key.

**You remember**: Around quarter to midnight, you stepped outside for some air near the kitchen entrance and noticed someone from the hotel staff leaving through the kitchen door — quickly, head down, hands in their jacket. You didn't think much of it at the time.

---

### THE 5 BEATS

**Beat 1 — Establishing**
The display case was found open at 12:05am by a security guard doing his rounds. No glass broken, no alarm triggered. The lock was opened with the correct key.

**Beat 2 — Narrowing**
Hotel management confirms only three keys to the display case exist. One was in the head of security's safe. One was with the exhibition coordinator, who was on stage giving a speech at the time. The third was kept in the night manager's office — and that office was unlocked all evening.

**Beat 3 — Tension**
CCTV from the main corridor shows no one entering or leaving the exhibition room between 11:30pm and midnight through the front entrance. But the room has a second door — leading to the kitchen service corridor. That camera was conveniently out of order.

**Beat 4 — Heat**
A kitchen porter says someone told him to take his break early that night — at 11:35pm, five minutes before the necklace disappeared. He can't remember who told him. The kitchen corridor would have been empty.

**Beat 5 — Near-damning**
Police searched staff lockers and vehicles this morning. Nothing was found in lockers. But one member of staff refused to open their car boot, saying they'd lost their keys. They were seen in the hotel car park at 11:50pm.

---

## STORY 2: THE BIRTHDAY MESSAGE

**Tone**: Social Drama — personal, uncomfortable

---

### CRIME SCENARIO
*Shown on TV at game start. All players see this.*

Last Saturday was Priya's 30th birthday party. Forty people, a rented bar, a photo wall, a playlist she'd spent weeks on. On Sunday morning, Priya found a group chat — one she'd never been added to — screenshotted and sent to her by an anonymous number. In it, six of her closest friends had been mocking her for months. Someone at the party leaked that chat on purpose. Someone wanted Priya to see.

---

### GUILTY BRIEF
*Private. Only the Guilty player reads this.*

You leaked the group chat. You've been in it since October — laughing along to keep the peace — but you've always felt sick about it. Two weeks ago, Priya helped you through something real, and you decided: she deserves to know. At the party, you screenshotted the chat from your phone, created a burner email, and sent the screenshots to Priya's number from a temporary online SMS service while sitting in the bathroom at 11pm. You used the bar's Wi-Fi so it couldn't be traced to your mobile data. At 4+ players: [ACCOMPLICE NAME] knows what you did — they kept people away from the bathroom corridor while you sent it.

---

### WITNESS BRIEF
*Private. Only the Witness player reads this. They do NOT know they are a Witness.*

Last Saturday was Priya's 30th birthday party. Forty people, a rented bar, a photo wall, a playlist she'd spent weeks on. On Sunday morning, Priya found a group chat — one she'd never been added to — screenshotted and sent to her by an anonymous number. In it, six of her closest friends had been mocking her for months. Someone at the party leaked that chat on purpose. Someone wanted Priya to see.

**You remember**: At about 11pm, you went to use the bathroom and someone was in there for ages — you could see the light of a phone screen under the door. You almost knocked but decided to wait. When they came out, they looked flustered and went straight back to the party without washing their hands.

---

### THE 5 BEATS

**Beat 1 — Establishing**
Priya received the screenshots at 11:07pm on Saturday, sent from an unrecognised number. The screenshots show a WhatsApp group called "Lol Priya" with messages going back to October. Six members, all at the party.

**Beat 2 — Narrowing**
The anonymous number was traced to a free online SMS service. No account needed — just a web browser and an internet connection. Whoever sent it didn't need a second phone. They just needed two minutes and Wi-Fi.

**Beat 3 — Tension**
The bar's Wi-Fi logs show a device connected at 10:58pm and disconnected at 11:04pm. That device wasn't connected before or after. It connected, did what it needed to do, and vanished from the network. The bar says the bathroom has the weakest signal but it still works.

**Beat 4 — Heat**
One of the six group chat members told Priya last week: "I never said anything mean, I just didn't leave the group." Priya scrolled back through the screenshots. That person's messages were mostly laughing emojis and "haha" — but they never pushed back. And two weeks before the party, they'd gone quiet in the chat entirely.

**Beat 5 — Near-damning**
Priya checked her phone records. The anonymous text arrived at 11:07pm. At 11:09pm, one person at the party texted Priya directly saying "Happy birthday babe, love you so much xx." She now thinks that text was guilt.

---

## STORY 3: THE Henderson REPORT

**Tone**: Workplace Scandal — dry, satirical

---

### CRIME SCENARIO
*Shown on TV at game start. All players see this.*

Someone has deleted the Henderson Report. This was the only copy of a 140-page internal audit that was due to go to the board on Monday. It vanished from the shared drive at 9:47pm on Friday night, long after the office had emptied out. The IT team confirmed: the file wasn't corrupted — it was deliberately moved to the recycle bin and then permanently deleted. Someone logged into the system remotely, found the file, and killed it.

---

### GUILTY BRIEF
*Private. Only the Guilty player reads this.*

You deleted the Henderson Report. Page 67 contained a line about "ichequote irregularities in Q3 expense submissions" — your expense submissions. You've been claiming mileage for client visits you never made. Nothing huge — maybe three thousand pounds over six months — but enough to get you fired. On Friday night at 9:45pm, you logged in from home using your work laptop, navigated to the shared drive, deleted the report, and emptied the recycle bin. You used your home Wi-Fi. You didn't think IT would check the logs. At 4+ players: [ACCOMPLICE NAME] told you the report mentioned expenses — they'd read an early draft.

---

### WITNESS BRIEF
*Private. Only the Witness player reads this. They do NOT know they are a Witness.*

Someone has deleted the Henderson Report. This was the only copy of a 140-page internal audit that was due to go to the board on Monday. It vanished from the shared drive at 9:47pm on Friday night, long after the office had emptied out. The IT team confirmed: the file wasn't corrupted — it was deliberately moved to the recycle bin and then permanently deleted. Someone logged into the system remotely, found the file, and killed it.

**You remember**: You were online late Friday doing some personal admin on the work VPN and you noticed the shared drive had an access notification pop up around quarter to ten. You saw the Henderson file was open. You thought someone was just working late but it seemed odd — nobody works on audit reports at that hour.

---

### THE 5 BEATS

**Beat 1 — Establishing**
IT has confirmed the deletion occurred at 9:47pm on Friday via remote access. The login credentials used belong to a current employee. The session lasted only four minutes — 9:44pm to 9:48pm. Whoever did this knew exactly where the file was.

**Beat 2 — Narrowing**
The Henderson Report covered five departments. IT recovered the table of contents from a cached backup. The sections flagged as "requiring board attention" were: Marketing budget allocation, Q3 expense submissions, and the Facilities contractor dispute. Someone had a reason to make this report disappear.

**Beat 3 — Tension**
HR has confirmed that one employee's VPN login was used for the deletion. That same VPN account was used earlier that day — at 2pm — to access the shared drive and open the Henderson Report in read-only mode. Someone read it first, then came back later to destroy it.

**Beat 4 — Heat**
The Henderson Report's author, Jan Henderson, kept handwritten notes. Her notes for page 67 read: "Mileage claims — pattern of visits to client sites with no corresponding meeting records. Approx. £3K over six months. Flag for board." This was in the Q3 expenses section. Someone's been over-claiming.

**Beat 5 — Near-damning**
IT pulled the building's Wi-Fi connection logs for Friday evening. At the office: no one was connected after 7pm. But the VPN login at 9:44pm came from a residential IP address. IT says they can match it to an employee's home broadband by Monday. The person who did this is in this room.

---

## DESIGN NOTES

**Witness escalation pattern**: In all three stories, the Witness's "truths" are innocuous in early rounds (Round 1-2: "I was getting fresh air", "I was on VPN Friday night") but become increasingly pointed by Rounds 3-5 ("Someone used that kitchen exit", "A four-minute session says panic"). The Witness doesn't know they're doing this — they're just answering honestly. But the Guilty player, who knows the full story, will notice.

**Guilty strategy**: The Guilty player knows every beat before it drops. Their optimal play is to seem normal early, then — once the Witness starts being too accurate — claim to be a Witness themselves. "I noticed something weird that night too." This forces a standoff between two people who both seem to know too much.

**5-word limit**: All example statements above are 5 words or fewer. This constraint forces players into punchy, ambiguous statements that are harder to interrogate — which is the point.

**Round prompts**: Each prompt is designed to be answerable by anyone (guilty, witness, or innocent) while creating natural openings for the Witness's knowledge to leak through and for the Guilty to deflect.
