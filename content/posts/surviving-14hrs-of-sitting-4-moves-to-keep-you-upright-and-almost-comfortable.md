You’ve woken up and you slept a bit weird. You’re neck hurts when you turn to your left. Your lower back has been a bit stiffer getting out of your chair lately. That headache is still like a rolling hum in the background.

Now you’re getting ready to push the rock up the hill again. _“I’ll try to get to out for a walk later”_ you say… you probably won’t get out for a walk later

You’ve spent more quality time with your office chair lately than with your family. Your chair is comfy enough but you’re always shifting to take pressure off the little niggly pains.

After 10 years of perfecting questionable posture (and paying for it later), I’ve picked up a few quick tricks worth sharing.

Four simple moves that’ll take you all of four minutes is whats on the menu. Nothing fancy — just super fast, super easy and practical solutions to keep you functioning like a normal human being.

> Give yourself a little shake out and we’ll get started.   
> For the first time, we can do this together.

### **1\. Take it easy on your jaw! _(30 seconds)_**

Chances are you’re clenching your jaw right now. Yes, right this second, stress-grinding away without even realizing it. It’s a stealthy stress habit, and most of us do it without even realising.

**The Fix:**  
\- Let your jaw relax (seriously, right now)  
\- Put your tongue behind your front teeth  
\- That’s literally it — easiest fix you’ll make all day

I bit you’re clenching your jaw already right?

### 2\. Roll your neck (1 minute)

When your neck feels about as flexible as a wooden plank, try this:

**The Fix:**  
\- Tilt your head toward one shoulder, nice and easy  
\- Hold for 15 seconds  
\- Drop your chin slightly  
\- Another 15 seconds  
\- Repeat on the other side, because symmetry matters

The goal here is to feel the stretch in the longer side. Head leans to the right, you should feel a nice stretch on the left. Same for front and back but ease into that one. **No one’s in a rush here and this one feels REALLY good**.

### 3\. Loosen those shoulders (1 minute)

Are they your shoulders creeping up to your ears again?

**The Fix:**  
\- Roll them forward three times  
\- Then backward three times  
\- On the last roll, keep them back and down  
\- Pinch your shoulder blades (Imagine holding a pencil between your shoulder blades)  
\- Ten seconds, twice more, and you’re done

Take your time and enjoy this one. For some reason, this one always feels great to me but my shoulders are a disaster.

### 4\. Save your spine (90 seconds)

For when your back is complaining about all that sitting:

**The Fix:**  
\- Sit tall in your chair  
\- Roll your spine back into a ‘C’ shape, rounding it out (think Cat pose)  
\- Hold for three seconds to really feel that stretch.  
\- Roll forward, arching your spine the other way (think Cow pose).  
\- Three rounds of that and you’re then you’re done for the day.

Think of this one as Cat / Cow but sitting.

### **Every little bit helps**

Look, none of these moves will drastically improve your strength and fitness. That’s not the point. They might be the difference between a day that’s dragging and one that’s actually decent. Between a day that rates a 3/10 and one at 5/10 day.

**Four minutes of your morning for a noticeably better day.   
That’s a pretty good deal.**

You don’t need to do this religiously (though your body wouldn’t complain if you did). Just make it part of your morning routine, before diving into whatever chaos awaits in your inbox.

> Think of it as running your car for 4 mins on a cold morning before setting off.

It’s basic maintenance for your body.

If anyone asks what you’re doing, just say you’re _“getting ready for a day of carrying you!_”   
They’ll either get it or leave you alone — win-win either way.

Oh, and unclench that jaw again — because we both know you just did.
